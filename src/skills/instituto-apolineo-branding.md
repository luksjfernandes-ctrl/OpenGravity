# Instituto Apolíneo — Brand Guidelines

## Overview

Instituto Apolíneo is a rhetoric and communication education company founded by Lucas Jonas Fernandes. It operates at the intersection of philosophy, power, and persuasion. The brand positions itself as a luxury editorial voice in the education space — closer to a fashion house than a coaching business.

Use this skill whenever producing any artifact that should reflect the Instituto Apolíneo identity: social media content, presentations, landing pages, documents, carousels, emails, course materials, or institutional communications.

**Keywords**: Instituto Apolíneo, Fale e Seja Ouvido, branding, luxury editorial, rhetoric, symbolic power, visual identity, brand voice, typography, brand colors, content style, communication, persuasion

## Brand Philosophy

### Core Thesis

> "Quem controla as narrativas controla o mundo."

The brand does NOT teach "public speaking." It teaches **narrative control** — the ability to shape perception, command authority, and make ideas land with force. The positioning is philosophical-subversive: it frames communication as a power structure, not a soft skill.

### Institutional Mantra

> "O mundo é produto de quem cria."

### Target Avatar

Technically competent professionals (lawyers, engineers, researchers, healthcare professionals, academics) who have deep expertise but struggle with workplace presence, authority in meetings, and making their ideas heard. They don't lack knowledge — they lack **symbolic power**.

### Brand Personality

- **Intellectual but not academic** — avoids jargon for jargon's sake; prefers sharp, clear provocation
- **Luxurious but not elitist** — the aesthetic signals sophistication, the message is democratizing
- **Philosophical but not abstract** — every concept must connect to a concrete transformation
- **Subversive but not aggressive** — challenges conventions with elegance, not hostility
- **Authoritative but warm** — commands respect through mastery, not distance

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette

**Primary Colors (Gucci-Inspired Luxury):**

- Apolíneo Black: `#1A1A1A` — Primary text, dark backgrounds, authority
- Apolíneo Ivory: `#F5F0E8` — Light backgrounds, paper texture, elegance
- Apolíneo Gold: `#C9A84C` — Primary accent, prestige, highlights, CTAs
- Apolíneo Deep Green: `#1B4332` — Secondary dark, depth, intellectual gravitas

**Secondary Colors:**

- Warm Cream: `#EDE5D8` — Subtle backgrounds, card surfaces
- Muted Olive: `#5C6B4F` — Tertiary accent, organic sophistication
- Charcoal: `#2D2D2D` — Secondary text, UI elements
- Soft Gold: `#D4B86A` — Lighter accent variation, borders, decorative elements

**Functional Colors:**

- Error/Alert: `#8B2E2E` — Deep burgundy (never bright red)
- Success: `#2D5A3D` — Forest green
- Info/Link: `#4A6FA5` — Muted steel blue

### Typography

**Headings:** Playfair Display (with Georgia fallback)
- Use for titles, section headers, hero text, carousel headlines
- Weight: Bold (700) for primary headings, SemiBold (600) for subheadings
- Style: Occasionally Italic for editorial flair on key phrases

**Body Text:** Inter (with Helvetica Neue / Arial fallback)
- Use for paragraphs, descriptions, UI text, captions
- Weight: Regular (400) for body, Medium (500) for emphasis
- Clean, modern, highly readable — contrasts with the classical headings

**Accent/Quotes:** Cormorant Garamond (with Times New Roman fallback)
- Use for pull quotes, philosophical citations, manifesto-style text
- Weight: Medium (500) or Medium Italic (500i)
- Evokes classical authority; use sparingly for maximum impact

**Monospace (if needed):** JetBrains Mono
- Only for code, technical references, or data displays

### Font Sizing Hierarchy (Web/Digital)

- Hero/Display: 48–64px (Playfair Display Bold)
- H1: 36–40px (Playfair Display Bold)
- H2: 28–32px (Playfair Display SemiBold)
- H3: 22–24px (Playfair Display SemiBold)
- Body Large: 18px (Inter Regular)
- Body: 16px (Inter Regular)
- Caption/Small: 13–14px (Inter Regular or Medium)
- Pull Quote: 24–28px (Cormorant Garamond Medium Italic)

## Voice & Tone

### Writing Style

The Apolíneo editorial voice is **ETER Brasil-inspired**: sophisticated, culturally dense, and visually striking. It reads like the intersection of a philosophy journal and a luxury magazine.

**DO:**
- Open with a provocation or counterintuitive claim
- Use short, punchy sentences for rhythm — then expand with depth
- Reference philosophy, history, art, and culture naturally (not pedantically)
- Write as if the reader is intelligent and ambitious
- Use metaphors drawn from power, architecture, theater, war, music
- End with a line that lingers — a closing that reframes the whole piece

**DON'T:**
- Use coaching clichés ("unlock your potential", "find your voice", "be authentic")
- Write in bullet-point-heavy listicle format for editorial content
- Use emojis in editorial/carousel content (acceptable in Stories or casual Reels captions)
- Default to corporate/motivational language
- Explain the obvious — trust the reader's intelligence
- Use "dicas" (tips) framing — the brand teaches frameworks, not tips

### Tone Spectrum

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Instagram Carousels | Editorial, provocative, dense — each slide is a page of a luxury magazine |
| Landing Pages | Authoritative, aspirational, clear value proposition |
| Course Materials | Rigorous but accessible, Socratic, example-rich |
| Emails/Newsletters | Intimate, direct, slightly conspiratorial (insider knowledge) |
| Institutional Docs | Professional, precise, philosophically grounded |
| Social Stories/Reels | Warmer, more personal, behind-the-scenes energy |

### Signature Linguistic Patterns

- Contrast structures: "Não é X. É Y." / "O problema não é [common assumption]. O problema é [reframe]."
- Philosophical anchoring: Brief references to thinkers (Habermas, Nietzsche, Aristóteles, Sloterdijk) woven naturally, never as name-dropping
- Power vocabulary: presença, autoridade simbólica, gravitas, narrativa, controle, enquadramento, domínio, precisão, impacto
- Avoid: "dicas", "hacks", "segredos", "método infalível", "passo a passo simples"

## Content Formats

### Instagram Carousels (Flagship Format)

- **Structure**: 7–10 slides, editorial density
- **Slide 1 (Cover)**: Bold provocative headline (Playfair Display), dark background (#1A1A1A) or deep green (#1B4332), gold accent element
- **Slides 2–8 (Content)**: One core idea per slide, ivory/cream backgrounds, black text, gold highlights on key phrases
- **Final Slide (CTA)**: Clear next step, institutional branding, gold accent
- **Visual style**: Clean, generous white space, no stock photos, typographic-driven design
- **Benchmark**: ETER Brasil editorial aesthetic

### Presentations / Slide Decks

- Dark theme preferred (Black #1A1A1A background, Ivory #F5F0E8 text)
- Gold (#C9A84C) for accents, dividers, key highlights
- One idea per slide — never cluttered
- Playfair Display for slide titles, Inter for body content
- Minimal imagery; when used, prefer architectural, classical art, or abstract textures

### Documents / PDFs

- Header: Playfair Display, Apolíneo Gold accent line
- Body: Inter, comfortable line height (1.6–1.8)
- Pull quotes: Cormorant Garamond Italic, indented with gold left border
- Footer: "Instituto Apolíneo" + mantra in small caps

### Font Management

- Uses Google Fonts (Playfair Display, Inter, Cormorant Garamond) for web
- For PPTX/PDF: falls back to Georgia (headings), Arial (body), Times New Roman (accent)
- No font installation required — fallback chain ensures readability
- For best results, pre-install Playfair Display, Inter, and Cormorant Garamond

### Color Application Rules

1. **Dark-on-light** (default): Black text on Ivory/Cream backgrounds, Gold accents
2. **Light-on-dark** (hero/impact): Ivory text on Black/Deep Green backgrounds, Gold accents
3. **Never** use Gold as a background color — it's always an accent (text highlights, borders, icons, dividers)
4. **Never** use pure white (#FFFFFF) or pure black (#000000) — always use the brand variants
5. Accent colors cycle: Gold → Deep Green → Olive (for multi-element layouts)

## Sub-brands

### Fale e Seja Ouvido (Program)

- Uses the same visual system as Instituto Apolíneo
- May emphasize Gold more prominently (as the "hero" program)
- Tagline positioning: "O programa para profissionais que sabem, mas não são ouvidos."
- Tone: slightly more direct and urgent than general Instituto content

### FACESB Integration (when applicable)

- When creating materials that bridge Instituto Apolíneo and FACESB (e.g., NPJ workshops with AoR methodology), use Apolíneo brand as primary, with FACESB logo/name as institutional co-branding
- FACESB materials that are purely institutional should NOT use the Apolíneo brand system
